About

Zhenhao Wen

Performer, Researcher, Choreographer

I'm in the process of shaping my voice as an artist. I work at the intersection of embodied knowledge, practice-as-research, and performance studies.

My research examines how dancers excluded from institutional archives create alternative knowledge systems through improvisation and emotional memory. Through practice-as-research methods including video documentation, movement analysis, and reflective writing, I study how bodies carry and transmit knowledge that resists conventional archiving.

Currently a Ph.D. candidate in Performance Studies at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa (GPA 4.0), advised by Dr. Lorenzo Perillo and Dr. Kara Jhalak Miller. I hold an M.Sc. in Dance Science from the University of Edinburgh and a B.A. in Dance Performance from Shanghai Theatre Academy. My training spans contemporary dance and Latin dance.

Artist Statement

I'm just an ordinary person,
but I want to be seen.

I am not extraordinary. I don't shine the way people expect. I am clumsy sometimes, fragile most of the time. And yet I want to be seen. Don't you? Don't you also long for the moments when you feel small, or broken, or almost invisible, to still be remembered?

We are told only the perfect deserves to last, that only the flawless should be kept. But I don't believe that. I think our raw, unfinished, trembling selves also matter. They carry truth. They carry memory.

So this is not a monument. It is a doorway. A space to hold what usually slips away. A place where the incomplete, the unpolished, the too-much and not-enough can breathe.

Practice as Research

PAR Journal

Fall 2025 ~ Spring 2026 Completed

Bodies Left Out: Toward an Emotional Archive

A practice-as-research project examining how dancers excluded from institutional archives create alternative knowledge systems through improvisation and emotional memory. Using video documentation, movement analysis, and reflective writing across 30+ participants, this work investigates what happens when the body becomes the archive itself. The resulting paper has been submitted to Dance Education in Practice and is currently under peer review.

Embodied Archives Improvisation Emotional Memory Peer-Reviewed

Spring 2026 Upcoming

Chinese Jingju and the Disciplined Body

An upcoming practice-as-research investigation into Chinese Jingju (Beijing Opera) as a site of embodied transmission and perceptual discipline. This project will examine how Jingju training produces a specific relationship between the performer's body, aesthetic convention, and cultural authority, and what that relationship reveals about knowledge systems embedded in classical Chinese performance forms.

Jingju Beijing Opera Embodied Transmission Chinese Performance

Spring 2026 Upcoming

Body Colonization: Movement, Power, and Perceptual Override

An upcoming practice-as-research project investigating how colonial logics inscribe themselves onto and through moving bodies. This work will explore the mechanisms by which training regimes, aesthetic hierarchies, and institutional choreography colonize the dancer's perceptual field, reshaping their relationship to sensation, expression, and selfhood.

Body Colonization Perceptual Override Power Decolonial Practice
