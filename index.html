About

My name is Zhenhao Wen,
born in 2000.

I'm in the process of shaping my voice as an artist. I am a performer, researcher, and choreographer working at the intersection of embodied knowledge, practice-as-research, and performance studies.

My research framework, "embodied grammar," examines how bodies create knowledge through movement and improvisation. I investigate perceptual dispossession in Chinese dance training and explore how embodied practices carry cultural memory across borders.

Currently a PhD student in Performance Studies at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa, I hold an M.Sc. in Dance Science from the University of Edinburgh and a B.A. in Dance Performance from Shanghai Theatre Academy. My training spans contemporary dance, martial arts, tai chi, and Latin dance.

Artist Statement

I'm just an ordinary person, but I want to be seen.

I am not extraordinary. I don't shine the way people expect. I am clumsy sometimes, fragile most of the time. And yet I want to be seen. Don't you? Don't you also long for the moments when you feel small, or broken, or almost invisible, to still be remembered?

We are told only the perfect deserves to last, that only the flawless should be kept. But I don't believe that. I think our raw, unfinished, trembling selves also matter. They carry truth. They carry memory.

So this is not a monument. It is a doorway. A space to hold what usually slips away. A place where the incomplete, the unpolished, the too-much and not-enough can breathe.

What is fragile is still worth keeping.

Practice as Research

PAR Journal

Spring 2025

Solo Improvisation Sessions, Honolulu

Seven weeks of solo improvisation recorded and analyzed as practice-as-research. Each session begins with a different entry point: breath, weight, peripheral vision, floor contact. The body is both subject and instrument. What emerges is not choreography but an archaeology of habits, resistances, and sudden discoveries.

Improvisation Embodied Grammar Video Analysis

Spring 2025

Perceptual Dispossession in Chinese Dance Training

Conference paper in development for the AAAS panel examining how Chinese dance pedagogy systematically reorganizes the student's perceptual field. The body learns to override its own signals. What is trained is not just movement but a particular relationship to sensation, pain, and authority.

Phenomenology Pedagogy Cultural Analysis

2024 ~ 2025

Festival à Corps, France

Observing and participating in international performance practices. Documenting the gap between trained bodies and untrained environments, between institutional choreography and improvised response.
